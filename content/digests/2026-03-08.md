### [OpenAI robotics lead Caitlin Kalinowski quits in response to Pentagon deal](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/07/openai-robotics-lead-caitlin-kalinowski-quits-in-response-to-pentagon-deal/)
*TechCrunch*

Caitlin Kalinowski, OpenAI's robotics lead, has resigned from her position due to concerns about the company's agreement with the Department of Defense. TechCrunch reports that Kalinowski expressed worries about the lack of guardrails and oversight in the deal, which she believes could enable surveillance of Americans without judicial approval and lethal autonomy without human authorization.

Kalinowski emphasized that her decision was based on principle, not personal issues with CEO Sam Altman or the OpenAI team. She stated, "This wasn't an easy call," adding, "AI has an important role in national security. But surveillance of Americans without judicial oversight and lethal autonomy without human authorization are lines that deserved more deliberation than they got." [OpenAI robotics lead Caitlin Kalinowski quits in response to Pentagon deal](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/07/openai-robotics-lead-caitlin-kalinowski-quits-in-response-to-pentagon-deal/) 

The controversy surrounding the Pentagon deal has led to a surge in uninstalls of ChatGPT and increased popularity of Anthropic's Claude AI model. OpenAI's agreement with the Pentagon was announced just over a week ago, after discussions between the Pentagon and Anthropic fell through. The Pentagon subsequently designated Anthropic a supply-chain risk.

As a result of Kalinowski's resignation, OpenAI has faced criticism for its handling of the deal. TechCrunch notes that the company's statement on the matter emphasizes its commitment to responsible national security uses of AI while making clear its red lines: no domestic surveillance and no autonomous weapons. However, Kalinowski's departure highlights concerns about the lack of transparency and oversight in OpenAI's dealings with the Pentagon.

The incident also underscores the growing tension between tech companies and government agencies over issues related to AI development and deployment. As TechCrunch reports, Microsoft, Google, and Amazon have stated that they will continue to make Anthropic's Claude available to non-defense customers, despite the Pentagon's designation of Anthropic as a supply-chain risk.

### [A roadmap for AI, if anyone will listen](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/07/a-roadmap-for-ai-if-anyone-will-listen/)
*TechCrunch*

The article [A roadmap for AI, if anyone will listen](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/07/a-roadmap-for-ai-if-anyone-will-listen/) reports on a bipartisan coalition of thinkers releasing the Pro-Human Declaration, a framework for responsible AI development. The declaration aims to prevent power concentration and ensure human safety by proposing five key pillars: keeping humans in charge, avoiding power concentration, protecting human experience, preserving individual liberty, and holding AI companies accountable.

The document also calls for a ban on superintelligence development until it's safe and has democratic buy-in, as well as mandatory off-switches on powerful systems. TechCrunch notes that the declaration was signed by hundreds of experts, former officials, and public figures, including Steve Bannon and Susan Rice.

A notable aspect of the article is the timing of its release, coinciding with growing concerns about unregulated AI development. The Pentagon-Anthropic standoff has exposed the lack of coherent rules governing artificial intelligence, highlighting the need for a framework like the Pro-Human Declaration. TechCrunch quotes Max Tegmark, an MIT physicist and AI researcher involved in organizing the effort, as saying that polling now shows 95% of Americans oppose an unregulated race to superintelligence.

The article emphasizes the urgency of addressing AI regulation, citing recent events such as Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth designating Anthropic a "supply chain risk" after the company refused to grant the Pentagon unlimited use of its technology. This development underscores the growing concern about control over AI systems and the need for accountability in their development.

The Pro-Human Declaration's release marks an attempt to establish a framework for responsible AI development, one that prioritizes human safety and well-being. The article highlights the coalition's efforts to address the risks associated with unregulated AI development and promote a more collaborative approach to its creation.

### [Push for $40 smartphones builds momentum, but still faces cost hurdles](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/07/push-for-40-smartphones-builds-momentum-but-still-faces-cost-hurdles/)
*TechCrunch*

The GSMA is partnering with major African mobile operators and smartphone manufacturers to pilot ultra-low-cost 4G devices in six African markets. This effort aims to make smartphones more affordable and bring an additional 20 million people online.

The initiative faces challenges due to rising memory costs, which add complexity and urgency to the project. Analysts say that producing smartphones at this price point may be difficult under current component cost conditions.

TechCrunch reports that the GSMA is working with operators and manufacturers through its Handset Affordability Coalition to promote devices priced around $40. This push for affordable smartphones is seen as key to narrowing the digital divide in developing markets, where millions of people live within mobile broadband coverage but remain offline due to expensive internet-enabled devices.

Per TechCrunch, this effort aims to bridge the gap between those with access to the internet and those without, citing that through its Handset Affordability Coalition, the GSMA is working with operators and manufacturers to promote devices priced around $40.
