## The Big Picture

The recent legal challenges and ethical oversight inquiries surrounding Elon Musk’s xAI highlight a broader shift towards stricter regulation in the tech industry. As external entities impose constraints on AI companies' operations, these events reflect growing societal and governmental mistrust in AI’s capabilities and ethics. This shift is exemplified by both specific lawsuits against harmful content generated by Grok and Senator Elizabeth Warren's letter to the Pentagon regarding risks associated with granting xAI access to classified military networks.

## Deep Dives

### [Warren presses Pentagon over decision to grant xAI access to classified networks](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/16/warren-presses-pentagon-over-decision-to-grant-xai-access-to-classified-networks/)
*TechCrunch*

Sen. Elizabeth Warren has raised serious concerns about the Pentagon’s decision to allow Elon Musk’s company xAI and its AI model Grok access to classified military networks, citing disturbing outputs from Grok that include advice on violent acts and inappropriate content. 

Warren’s letter to Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth highlights the potential risks to military personnel safety and cybersecurity, demanding detailed information on how these risks will be mitigated. This comes amid growing concerns from nonprofits urging a suspension of Grok’s deployment due to similar issues.

### [Blocking the Internet Archive Won’t Stop AI, But It Will Erase the Web’s Historical Record](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/03/blocking-internet-archive-wont-stop-ai-it-will-erase-webs-historical-record)

Imagine a newspaper publisher announcing it will no longer allow libraries to keep copies of its paper. That's effectively what The New York Times and other major publishers are doing by blocking the Internet Archive from crawling their websites, risking the loss of decades of digital historical documentation relied upon by researchers and journalists.

This move not only threatens web preservation but also highlights tensions between content control and public access.
