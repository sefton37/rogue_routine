### [Anthropic won’t budge as Pentagon escalates AI dispute](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/anthropic-wont-budge-as-pentagon-escalates-ai-dispute/)
*TechCrunch*

The Pentagon's move is seen as an attempt to exert pressure on Anthropic to compromise on its stance against mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has argued that the military's use of technology should be governed by U.S. law and constitutional limits, not by private contractors' usage policies.

However, this position is met with skepticism from experts who warn that invoking the DPA would mark a significant expansion of its modern use. Dean Ball, senior fellow at the Foundation for American Innovation, notes that such an action would essentially allow the government to put companies out of business if they disagree with their "politically" driven policies.

The dispute highlights the tension between AI companies and the government over issues of national security and ethics. Anthropic's stance has been criticized by some in the administration, who have accused the company of having "woke" safety policies. The situation is seen as a high-stakes game of chicken, with both sides refusing to budge.

What this article does not reveal is the full extent of the Pentagon's plans for AI development and deployment. While it mentions the Defense Production Act, it does not delve into the specifics of how the government intends to use Anthropic's technology or what safeguards are in place to prevent its misuse. Additionally, the article does not explore the potential consequences of invoking the DPA on the broader tech industry.

### [Trump Administration Makes The Conscious Choice To Make America Less Prepared For The Next Pandemic](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/02/24/trump-administration-makes-the-conscious-choice-to-make-america-less-prepared-for-the-next-pandemic/)
*Tech Dirt*

The Trump administration has deliberately shifted focus away from pandemic research at the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID), despite its crucial role in responding to COVID-19. This decision will leave Americans more vulnerable to future outbreaks, as the agency's proactive efforts are being dismantled.

According to Tech Dirt, this move is a consequence of Trump's reelection efforts being damaged by his handling of the pandemic. By deprioritizing pandemic research, the administration is essentially ignoring lessons learned from COVID-19 and prioritizing basic immunology over preparedness for future pandemics. Experts warn that this shift will have devastating consequences.

Tech Dirt reports that NIH director Jay Bhattacharya explained the restructure at an event with other top agency officials on January 30th, stating that it's a "complete transformation" of NIAID away from its historical focus on HIV, biodefense, and pandemic preparedness. Instead, the institute will focus more on basic immunology and infectious diseases currently affecting people in the US.

This decision is significant because it highlights the administration's willingness to compromise public health for political gain. By deprioritizing pandemic research, the Trump administration is essentially betting that future pandemics won't occur or won't be severe enough to warrant attention. This gamble puts American lives at risk and undermines the agency's ability to respond effectively to emerging threats.

*TechCrunch*

Multiverse Computing, a Spanish startup, has released a compressed version of its HyperNova 60B large language model, making it more accessible to developers and potentially reducing costs for companies deploying AI solutions. The updated model, called HyperNova 60B 2602, is available for free on Hugging Face and boasts lower memory usage and latency compared to OpenAI's gpt-oss-120b.

This development is significant because it could democratize access to advanced AI models, allowing more developers to build and deploy AI-powered applications. Multiverse's compression technology, CompactifAI, has been applied to its model, making it roughly half the size of the original while maintaining similar accuracy and potency.
