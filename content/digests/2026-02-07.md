### [Pluralistic: End of the line for video essays (07 Feb 2026)](https://pluralistic.net/2026/02/07/aimsters-revenge/)
*Interconnects*

According to Interconnects, a recent article highlights how Section 1201 of the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) is being used to restrict access to copyrighted works, effectively turning any conduct that displeases a business into a felony. This law makes it a crime to bypass an "effective access control" for a copyrighted work, which can be as simple as altering software on a device.

The article reveals how this vague and restrictive law is having a chilling effect on the creation of video essays, a form of digital content that relies heavily on copyrighted materials. By making it a felony to circumvent access controls, businesses are essentially able to dictate what kind of content can be created and shared online, stifling creativity and innovation.

This story matters because it shows how powerful corporations are using the law to exert control over the internet and limit free expression. The DMCA's Section 1201 is a prime example of how laws can be used to favor business interests over individual rights and freedoms.

This quote underscores the arbitrary nature of the CIA's decision and the loss of valuable information.

### [Here’s how Roblox’s age checks work](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/06/heres-how-robloxs-age-checks-work/)
*TechCrunch*

According to TechCrunch, Roblox's implementation of facial verification and age checks is a notable step towards addressing concerns around child safety on its platform. The company uses third-party vendor Persona for facial verification, which deletes images and videos after processing. This raises questions about the effectiveness of such measures in preventing abuse.

The article highlights the ongoing efforts by Roblox to address loopholes in its age-check system, with 45% of daily active users having undergone age checks as of January 31. However, it also notes that users can appeal incorrect age estimates through alternative methods, including ID verification or parental controls. This suggests a tension between ensuring user safety and maintaining platform accessibility.

The article is noteworthy because it reveals the complexities of implementing effective age-check systems on online platforms. It highlights the challenges of balancing child safety with user autonomy and the need for continuous improvement in such measures.
