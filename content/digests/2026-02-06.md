### [Yes to the “ICE Out of Our Faces Act”](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/02/yes-ice-out-my-face-act)
*EFF*

What happens when government agencies are given unchecked power to use facial recognition technology on immigrants? The EFF reports that Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and Customs and Border Protection (CBP) have been using face recognition technology to scan faces of people suspected of unlawful presence in the country, with over 100,000 scans reported by the Wall Street Journal.

The EFF is advocating for the "ICE Out of Our Faces Act", a federal bill that would ban ICE and CBP agents from acquiring or using biometric surveillance systems, including face recognition technology. The bill's lead author, Senator Ed Markey, has introduced similar legislation in the past to ban all federal law enforcement agencies from using biometric surveillance systems.

The article highlights the dangers of facial recognition technology, citing a case where the technology misidentified a woman twice. EFF argues that government agencies should not use this technology at all, especially when it comes to immigration enforcement. The bill would also provide a private right of action for individuals whose rights are violated by the misuse of biometric surveillance systems.

As EFF explains, "Face recognition technology is so dangerous that government should not use it at all—least of all these out-of-control immigration agencies." This statement emphasizes the urgency and severity of the issue, highlighting the potential harm caused by unchecked use of facial recognition technology.
