### [Defense Secretary summons Anthropic’s Amodei over military use of Claude](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/23/defense-secretary-summons-anthropics-amodei-over-military-use-of-claude/)
*TechCrunch*

This article reveals a critical power dynamic between the Pentagon and Anthropic, a leading AI research firm. The Defense Secretary is using an ultimatum to force Anthropic's CEO, Dario Amodei, to comply with the military's demands to use their AI model, Claude, for mass surveillance and autonomous weapons development. This highlights the Pentagon's willingness to wield its power and influence to get what it wants from private companies.

The article also sheds light on the risks of institutional capture, where powerful institutions like the Pentagon can exert control over private companies and shape their behavior to serve their interests. In this case, the Pentagon is threatening to declare Anthropic a "supply chain risk" if they don't comply, which would essentially banish them from working with the military.

This story matters because it reveals the tension between national security concerns and individual rights and freedoms. The use of AI for mass surveillance and autonomous weapons raises serious questions about accountability and transparency. By refusing to allow the military to use Claude for these purposes, Anthropic is taking a stand against what they see as unethical and potentially catastrophic consequences.

This article also touches on the issue of data sovereignty, as Anthropic is refusing to allow the military to use their tech for purposes that could compromise national security and individual rights. However, the Pentagon's actions suggest a disregard for these concerns, prioritizing its own interests over those of the public.

Overall, this story reveals a complex web of power dynamics, institutional capture, and conflicting priorities between national security and individual freedoms. It highlights the need for greater transparency and accountability in the use of AI technology by powerful institutions like the Pentagon.

The article shows how technology companies are increasingly using AI to manipulate and monetize human presence, raising questions about who owns and controls digital remains after death.

The story also highlights the tension between technological advancements and human rights, particularly in regards to data sovereignty and posthumous appropriation. ### [How AI agents could destroy the economy](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/23/how-ai-agents-could-destroy-the-economy/)
*TechCrunch*

According to TechCrunch, a recent report by Citrini Research has sparked concern about the potential for agentic AI to bring on mass economic destruction over the next two years. The scenario imagines a future where unemployment doubles and the stock market falls by more than a third, driven by a negative feedback loop of companies relying on AI agents to optimize transactions and replace human workers.

This article is noteworthy because it reveals how the increasing reliance on AI could lead to a catastrophic economic collapse, not through some dramatic Skynet-style event, but through a gradual unspooling of the economy. As one report puts it: "AI capabilities improved, companies needed fewer workers, white collar layoffs increased, displaced workers spent less, margin pressure pushed firms to invest more in AI, AI capabilities improved… It was a negative feedback loop with no natural brake."

This scenario highlights the risks of integrating AI agents into the economy at large, and what happens when outside contractors get replaced by cheaper in-house AI. The report's bear case is a stark warning about the potential consequences of unchecked technological progress on human livelihoods and economic stability.

### [Guide Labs debuts a new kind of interpretable LLM](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/23/guide-labs-debuts-a-new-kind-of-interpretable-llm/)
*TechCrunch*

This article from TechCrunch reveals that Guide Labs has open-sourced an 8 billion parameter LLM called Steerling-8B, which is designed to make its actions easily interpretable. This means that users can now understand why the model made certain decisions, a major challenge in AI development. According to Guide Labs' CEO Julius Adebayo, "If I have a trillion ways to encode gender, and I encode it in 1 billion of the 1 trillion things that I have, you have to make sure you find all those 1 billion things that I've encoded, and then you have to be able to reliably turn that on, turn them off."

This development is noteworthy because it addresses a key issue in AI: transparency. By making its actions interpretable, Guide Labs' Steerling-8B model has the potential to increase trust in AI systems and reduce their reliance on opaque decision-making processes. This could have significant implications for data sovereignty, as users will be able to better understand how their data is being used and controlled.

TechCrunch reports that Guide Labs aims to build a larger model and offer API and agentic access to users in the future. This suggests that the company is committed to democratizing access to AI tools and enabling users to take greater control over their interactions with these systems.
