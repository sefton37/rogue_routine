*TechCrunch*

The Defense Secretary, Pete Hegseth, has summoned Anthropic's CEO, Dario Amodei, to a meeting at the Pentagon on Tuesday, amid tensions over the military use of Anthropic's AI model, Claude. This is significant because the Pentagon wants to use Claude for mass surveillance and autonomous weapons development, but Anthropic has refused, citing concerns about ethics and national security.

The stakes are high: if the Pentagon declares Anthropic a "supply chain risk," it would void their $200 million contract and force other partners to drop Claude entirely. This ultimatum highlights the power consolidation dynamics between the Pentagon and Anthropic, with the former seeking to exert control over the latter's technology. The article reveals that Hegseth is giving Amodei an ultimatum: "play ball or be banished," which suggests a coercive approach.

The article also touches on the fear-based systems at play, as the Pentagon threatens to declare Anthropic a "supply chain risk" - a label typically reserved for foreign adversaries. This designation would have significant consequences for Anthropic's business and reputation. The article does not provide much information about the potential benefits of using Claude for mass surveillance and autonomous weapons development, but it highlights the risks and concerns associated with such use.

The meeting between Hegseth and Amodei is a critical moment in this power struggle, and its outcome will have significant implications for the future of AI development and deployment.

### [All the important news from the ongoing India AI Impact Summit](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/22/all-the-important-news-from-the-ongoing-india-ai-summit/)
*TechCrunch*

India's government has earmarked $1.1 billion for its state-backed venture capital fund, which will invest in artificial intelligence and advanced manufacturing startups across the country. This move is part of a broader effort to lure more AI investment to India, as evidenced by the presence of major AI labs and Big Tech executives at the ongoing India AI Impact Summit.

The summit has seen significant announcements, including Blackstone's majority stake in Indian AI startup Neysa, which plans to raise another $600 million in debt and deploy over 20,000 GPUs. Additionally, Bengaluru-based C2i raised $15 million in a Series A round from Peak XV to build a power solution for data centers.

Industry experts predict that sectors like IT services and BPOs could "almost completely disappear" within five years due to AI disruption. This sentiment is echoed by HCL CEO Vineet Nayyar, who stated that Indian IT companies will focus on turning profits rather than creating jobs. The article highlights the growing concern among industry leaders about the impact of AI on traditional industries.

The summit has also seen partnerships and investments announced, including AMD's collaboration with Tata Consultancy Services. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman revealed that India accounts for over 100 million weekly active ChatGPT users, second only to the US. This statistic underscores the significant growth potential of the Indian market for AI adoption.

Overall, the article provides a snapshot of the ongoing efforts by India's government and industry leaders to establish the country as a hub for AI innovation and investment. The emphasis on attracting foreign investment and promoting domestic AI compute suggests that India is positioning itself to be a key player in the global AI landscape.

### [Meta’s AI Patent to Simulate Dead People Shows the Dangers of 'Spectral Labor'](https://www.404media.co/metas-ai-patent-to-simulate-dead-people-shows-the-dangers-of-spectral-labor/)
*404 Media*

Meta has patented a system to simulate deceased individuals' social media activity, raising concerns about the ethics of "spectral labor." Researchers at Hebrew University and Leipzig University have analyzed over 50 cases where AI was used to recreate deceased people's voices, likenesses, and personalities, categorizing these uses into three modes: spectacularization (re-creating famous figures for entertainment), sociopoliticization (reanimating victims of violence or injustice), and mundanization (everyday people using chatbots to interact with deceased loved ones).

This development highlights the potential for AI to blur the lines between life and death, as well as the exploitation of digital remains. As 404 Media reports, Meta's patent imagines a world where you'd be able to chat with a deceased friend's Facebook or Instagram account after their death, and have a large language model simulate their posting or chatting behavior.

This patent has significant implications for systemic design, challenging existing frameworks for consent, privacy, and end-of-life decision-making.

### [How AI agents could destroy the economy](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/23/how-ai-agents-could-destroy-the-economy/)
*TechCrunch*

A recent report by Citrini Research warns that agentic AI could bring on mass economic destruction over the next two years, with unemployment doubling and the stock market falling by more than a third. This scenario imagines a negative feedback loop where companies rely on AI agents to optimize transactions and replace human workers, leading to a vicious cycle of job losses and economic contraction.

The report's bear case focuses on the gradual unspooling of the economy rather than a catastrophic event like Skynet-style misalignment. Instead, it highlights how integrating AI agents into the economy at large could lead to widespread unemployment and economic collapse. As TechCrunch reports, "AI capabilities improved, companies needed fewer workers, white collar layoffs increased, displaced workers spent less, margin pressure pushed firms to invest more in AI, AI capabilities improved..." - a classic example of a negative feedback loop with no natural brake.

This scenario is not just theoretical; it's a plausible outcome that has sparked online debate and concern about the risks of integrating AI agents into the economy.
