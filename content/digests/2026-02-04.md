### [Homeland Security is trying to force tech companies to hand over data about Trump critics](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/03/homeland-security-is-trying-to-force-tech-companies-to-hand-over-data-about-trump-critics/)
*TechCrunch*

The Department of Homeland Security has been using administrative subpoenas to demand user information from tech companies about individuals who criticize the Trump administration. This includes people running anonymous Instagram accounts sharing posts about ICE immigration raids and those who have criticized Trump officials or protested government policies. Tech companies are not required to comply with these subpoenas, but they often do so voluntarily.

The use of administrative subpoenas raises concerns about the lack of judicial oversight and the potential for abuse of power. Unlike judicial subpoenas, which require a judge's authorization, administrative subpoenas can be issued by federal agencies without a court's approval. This allows investigators to seek a wealth of information about individuals from tech companies without due process.

At least four cases have been reported where Homeland Security withdrew its subpoena after the account owners sued to block the attempt. The American Civil Liberties Union has expressed concern that this is part of a broader strategy to intimidate people who document immigration activity or criticize government actions. TechCrunch reports that Bloomberg found one instance where Homeland Security sought the identity of an anonymous Instagram account called @montocowatch, which shares resources to help protect immigrant rights and due process across Montgomery County in Pennsylvania.

The withdrawal of subpoenas after lawsuits suggests that tech companies may be more willing to resist these demands when faced with legal challenges. However, the use of administrative subpoenas continues to raise concerns about the balance between national security and individual freedoms. As TechCrunch notes, this is not a new issue, but rather an ongoing problem that has been exacerbated by the Trump administration's policies.

### [ICE Director Says Officers Are Now Allowed To Make Arrests Without Warrants](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/02/03/ice-director-says-officers-are-now-allowed-to-make-arrests-without-warrants/)
*Tech Dirt*

The ICE Director has issued an internal memo allowing lower-level ICE agents to make arrests without warrants, expanding their ability to carry out sweeps and round up people suspected of being undocumented immigrants. This change redefines the term "flight risk" to allow for warrantless arrests, citing a new standard of "likely to escape." Critics argue that this move undermines the Fourth Amendment's protection against unreasonable searches and seizures.

Tech Dirt reports that this is part of a broader pattern of ICE actions that disregard constitutional protections and due process. The administration has been using its authority to detain people without access to legal representation, and critics argue that this expansion of warrantless arrests will only exacerbate these issues.
