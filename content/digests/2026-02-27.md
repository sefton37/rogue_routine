### [Victory! Tenth Circuit Finds Fourth Amendment Doesn’t Support Broad Search of Protesters’ Devices and Digital Data](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/02/victory-tenth-circuit-finds-fourth-amendment-doesnt-support-broad-search-0)
*EFF*

A court has ruled that police warrants to search protesters' devices and digital data are unconstitutional, a significant win for protesters' rights.

The ruling in Armendariz v. City of Colorado Springs came after police arrested protesters during a 2021 housing protest and obtained warrants to seize and search their devices and data. The warrants were deemed overbroad and lacked particularity, violating "clearly established" law and denying qualified immunity to the officers involved.

This decision is notable because it questions the constitutionality of sweeping search warrants and denies police immunity from lawsuits for such actions. It also highlights concerns about police surveillance and manipulation of protesters' devices during demonstrations.

The case has implications for how law enforcement agencies balance their need to gather evidence with individuals' right to privacy, especially in the context of protests and free speech.
