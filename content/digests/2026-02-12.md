### [More US investors sue South Korean government over handling of Coupang data breach](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/12/more-u-s-investors-sue-south-korean-government-over-handling-of-coupang-data-breach/)
*TechCrunch*

A significant development has emerged in the ongoing saga surrounding Coupang's massive data breach, which exposed nearly 34 million customers' personal information. TechCrunch reports that more US investors have joined a lawsuit against the South Korean government, alleging discriminatory treatment and unfair penalties.

The investors, including Abrams Capital, Durable Capital Partners, and Foxhaven Asset Management, claim that the government's actions were unlawful and resulted in losses for them. They are seeking international arbitration under the US-Korea Free Trade Agreement (FTA) to resolve the dispute. This move highlights the growing tension between the South Korean government and Coupang's investors, who feel that the government has unfairly targeted the company.

The data breach itself was a major incident, with customer names, email addresses, phone numbers, shipping addresses, and certain order histories compromised. While other tech breaches in Korea have resulted in less severe penalties, Coupang has faced extraordinary pressure from the government, including threatened fines, suspension of operations, and travel bans for executives.

TechCrunch notes that the government's actions against Coupang have been criticized as excessive and unfair. The investors' decision to seek international arbitration suggests that they believe the government's treatment of Coupang was discriminatory and in violation of the FTA. This development has significant implications for the relationship between the South Korean government and foreign investors, particularly those from the US.

The lawsuit also raises questions about the balance between data security and economic interests. The South Korean government's actions against Coupang have been motivated by concerns over data protection, but the investors' complaint suggests that these measures may have gone too far. This case highlights the challenges of regulating data breaches in a global economy, where companies like Coupang operate across borders.
