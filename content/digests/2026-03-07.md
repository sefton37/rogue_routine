**The Pentagon's Designation of Anthropic as a Supply-Chain Risk: A Watershed Moment in Corporate Accountability**

Anthropic's refusal to grant unrestricted access to its AI models for military use has led the Pentagon to designate the company as a supply-chain risk, marking a significant turning point in the attention economy's struggle to balance corporate accountability and government control. This development reveals the tension between Anthropic's stance on data sovereignty and the Pentagon's demands for access to sensitive technologies. The story highlights the consequences of this power struggle, including the potential misuse of AI in military contexts.

This story matters more than others today because it exposes the complexities of working with the military and the need for startups to carefully navigate these relationships. Anthropic's stance on data sovereignty is a critical aspect of its business model, and the Pentagon's designation sends a strong signal that companies prioritizing transparency and accountability may face significant challenges.

### [Microsoft, Google, Amazon say Anthropic Claude remains available to non-defense customers](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/06/microsoft-anthropic-claude-remains-available-to-customers-except-the-defense-department/)
*TechCrunch*

The Pentagon has designated Anthropic, a US-based AI startup, as a supply-chain risk due to its refusal to grant unrestricted access to its technology for military use. This move is significant because it typically reserves this designation for foreign adversaries.

Microsoft and Google have confirmed that their customers can continue using Anthropic's AI model Claude for non-defense purposes despite the Pentagon's decision. Microsoft stated that they will continue making Claude available through their platforms, while Amazon's AWS customers can also keep using the model for non-defense workloads.

The key takeaway is that major tech companies are standing by Anthropic and its AI model, even in the face of a high-profile designation from the US government. This suggests that these companies value data sovereignty (the ability to control one's own data) over potential military contracts or collaborations with the Pentagon.

In this context, it's worth noting that Anthropic has vowed to fight the designation in court, citing concerns over mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. The company's stance is likely to resonate with those who prioritize transparency and accountability in AI development.

TechCrunch reports that Microsoft sells an array of products to many federal agencies, including the Defense Department, but will continue making Claude available to its customers - except for the Department of War. Google has also confirmed that it will continue to make Claude available to its customers.

The Pentagon's designation is a clear attempt to exert control over Anthropic and its technology, highlighting concerns about data sovereignty and the potential misuse of AI in military contexts. The tech giants' support for Anthropic sends a strong signal that they prioritize the company's values and principles over government pressure.
