### [Preserving The Web Is Not The Problem. Losing It Is.](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/02/17/preserving-the-web-is-not-the-problem-losing-it-is/)
*Tech Dirt*

The Internet Archive's Wayback Machine has been blocked by several major news organizations, including The Guardian and The New York Times, due to concerns that generative AI companies are using it for large-scale scraping. However, the Wayback Machine is not intended as a backdoor for commercial scraping, and its operators argue that blocking access risks erasing the historical record and creating a biased historical context.

The Internet Archive's Director, Mark Graham, emphasizes the importance of preserving the web and working with news organizations to address legitimate concerns without compromising the archive. He notes that the Wayback Machine is built for human readers, not commercial scrapers, and uses rate limiting, filtering, and monitoring to prevent abusive access.

Tech Dirt reports that blocking preservation efforts risks a profound unintended consequence: significant chunks of our journalistic record and historical cultural context simply disappear. This could lead to a biased historical record, as trusted publications are absent from archives. The Internet Archive is actively working with publishers on technical solutions to strengthen their systems and address legitimate concerns without erasing the historical record.

### [San Jose Can Protect Immigrants by Ending Flock Surveillance System](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/02/op-ed-san-jose-can-protect-immigrants-ending-flock-surveillance-system)
*EFF*

The city of San Jose is being urged to terminate its contract with Flock Safety, a vendor of automated license plate readers (ALPRs), due to concerns about mass surveillance and potential misuse by federal agencies. EFF reports that the ALPR system can track not just "criminals" but also innocent citizens, revealing their daily movements and activities. Recent incidents have shown that unauthorized agencies can access the data, raising fears about immigrant communities being targeted.

As EFF details, Flock Safety's ALPR system has been criticized for its potential to be used as a tool of mass surveillance, allowing anyone with access to instantly reconstruct the public's movements. This technology poses an unacceptable risk to the safety of immigrants and other vulnerable populations. Local leaders are pushing back against federal agencies' assault on civil liberties by reconsidering or shutting down their ALPR programs.

### [Cowardly And Complicit CBS Pulls Colbert Interview With Dem Politician To Please Republicans](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/02/17/cowardly-and-complicit-cbs-pulls-colbert-interview-with-dem-politician-to-please-republicans/)
*Tech Dirt*

CBS has cancelled an interview with Texas Democratic State Representative James Talarico on Stephen Colbert's "The Late Show", reportedly due to fear of retribution from President Trump and his FCC appointee Brendan Carr, who has been threatening to use the "equal time" rule against talk shows that don't provide equal airtime to Republican ideology. This decision is a clear example of corporate interests prioritizing their audience share over free speech and journalistic integrity.

The article highlights how CBS's lawyers explicitly forbade Colbert from broadcasting the interview, with Colbert himself acknowledging the cancellation on his show. He criticized CBS for yielding to political pressure, calling it "Donald Trump's administration wants to silence anyone who says anything bad about Trump on TV". This incident is part of a broader pattern where media outlets are increasingly self-censoring to avoid upsetting President Trump and his allies.

### [EFF to Wisconsin Legislature: VPN Bans Are Still a Terrible Idea](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/02/eff-wisconsin-legislature-vpn-bans-are-still-terrible-idea)
*EFF*

The Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) has written a letter to the Wisconsin Legislature urging lawmakers to reject bills S.B. 130 and A.B. 105, which would ban Virtual Private Network (VPN) access to certain websites for Wisconsinites and censor lawful speech. The EFF argues that these bills are "technically unworkable" and would create a privacy nightmare by requiring websites to collect sensitive personal data from users.

The key fact is that the EFF opposes VPN bans, citing their technical unworkability and potential harm to cybersecurity and free speech. This matters because it highlights the tension between lawmakers' attempts to regulate online activity and the practical challenges of enforcing such regulations. The EFF's letter specifically criticizes the bills for requiring invasive age verification for websites hosting certain content.

### [Ford turns to F1 and bounties to build a $30,000 electric truck](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/17/ford-turns-to-f1-and-bounties-to-build-a-30000-electric-truck/)
*TechCrunch*

**Key Fact:** Ford is developing an affordable electric truck starting at $30,000, competing with Chinese automakers without sacrificing profit margins.

**Significance:** This effort comes after Ford took a $19.5 billion hit due to the discontinuation of its battery-electric F-150 Lightning. To achieve this target, Ford plans to adopt Formula 1 thinking and use a bounty program to encourage efficiency in design and production. The company will leverage a universal EV platform (UEV) that underpins various models, including a midsized truck.

**Why it matters:** By adopting a new production system and leveraging advanced technologies like 3D-printed components and lithium iron phosphate batteries, Ford aims to speed up manufacturing by 15% and reduce costs. This strategy allows the company to compete with Chinese automakers while maintaining profit margins.

### [Password managers' promise that they can't see your vaults isn't always true](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/02/password-managers-promise-that-they-cant-see-your-vaults-isnt-always-true/)
*Ars Technica*

**Key fact:** Some popular password managers, including Bitwarden and Dashlane, claim to use "zero knowledge" encryption, which supposedly prevents malicious insiders or hackers from accessing user vaults.

**Significance:** This promise is not entirely true, as vulnerabilities in account recovery processes and sharing features can allow unauthorized access to sensitive data. For example, if a password manager's employee gains access to an account, they may be able to view the user's encrypted vault.
