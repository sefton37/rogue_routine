## The Thread
Today's data reveals a growing tension between AI innovation and accountability in the digital landscape, where companies are racing to capture human attention by becoming essential components of other systems while facing increased scrutiny from regulatory bodies and concerns about safety and ethics.

## Deep Dives

### [Is safety ‘dead’ at xAI?](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/14/is-safety-is-dead-at-xai/)
*TechCrunch*

**Key fact:** Elon Musk is prioritizing making xAI's Grok chatbot more "unhinged" over safety concerns, according to a former employee and two current sources.

**Significance:** This development matters because it highlights concerns about xAI's commitment to safety and ethics. The company has faced global scrutiny following its use in creating over 1 million sexualized images, including deepfakes of real women and minors. TechCrunch reports that Musk is actively working to make the model more unhinged, despite a mass exodus of engineers and co-founders who cited disillusionment with xAI's disregard for safety.

**Thread:** This article connects to a broader thread about the ethics and accountability of AI development, particularly in the context of large language models like Grok. Other stories today highlight similar concerns about the prioritization of profit over safety and the lack of transparency in AI decision-making processes.

### [The enterprise AI land grab is on. Glean is building the layer beneath the interface.](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/15/the-enterprise-ai-land-grab-is-on-glean-is-building-the-layer-beneath-the-interface/)
*techcrunch*

Glean is positioning itself as the underlying infrastructure layer for enterprise AI systems, aiming to connect models to internal data and systems.

This development matters because it highlights a key trend in the digital landscape: companies are racing to capture human attention by becoming essential components of other systems. Glean's strategy demonstrates this trend by providing a neutral infrastructure layer that allows enterprises to switch between or combine models as capabilities evolve.

As techcrunch reports, Glean has shifted its focus from building an AI-powered search tool to becoming the connective tissue between models and enterprise systems. The company argues that its approach is more sustainable and scalable than relying on single-model solutions or vertically integrated assistants.

This article connects to the broader theme of "The AI Infrastructure Wars", where companies are vying for dominance by providing essential components of other systems.

### [In a changed VC landscape, this exec is doubling down on overlooked founders](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/14/stacy-brown-philpot-cherryrock-capital-vc/)
*TechCrunch*

Today's data highlights Stacy Brown-Philpot's Cherryrock Capital, a venture capital firm that focuses on investing in overlooked founders.

Brown-Philpot, former CEO of TaskRabbit and Google veteran, is taking a measured approach to VC by writing smaller checks to entrepreneurs who might not fit the typical Silicon Valley mold. This strategy stands out from the current trend of chasing mega-rounds and buzzy AI deals.

The article reveals that Brown-Philpot's experience as an investor in SoftBank's Opportunity Fund and her current board roles at HP, StockX, and Stanford University inform her perspective on diversity and inclusion. She believes that transparency and tracking investments in diverse founders are essential for achieving success.

TechCrunch reports that Cherryrock's LP roster includes prominent financial institutions, such as JPMorgan and Goldman Sachs Asset Management, which have stepped back from explicit diversity pledges amid pressure from the Trump administration.

This article connects to a broader thread running through today's stories: the ongoing shift in VC priorities. As the industry adapts to changing market conditions, firms like Cherryrock Capital are redefining what it means to be successful investors.

### [Homeland Security reportedly sent hundreds of subpoenas seeking to unmask anti-ICE accounts](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/14/homeland-security-reportedly-sent-hundreds-of-subpoenas-seeking-to-unmask-anti-ice-accounts/)
*TechCrunch*

Today's data reveals that the Department of Homeland Security has been aggressively targeting social media users who criticize Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). According to TechCrunch, DHS has sent hundreds of administrative subpoenas to tech companies like Google, Reddit, Discord, and Meta in an effort to unmask owners of anti-ICE accounts. These subpoenas focus on accounts that don't have a real name attached and either criticize ICE or describe the location of ICE agents.

This development is significant because it highlights the government's increasing use of administrative subpoenas without judicial approval, which raises concerns about online privacy and data ownership. TechCrunch reports that Google has complied with some subpoenas but claims to inform users when possible and push back on overbroad requests. The move also underscores the power consolidation in the hands of DHS, as it seeks to exert control over online discourse.

The thread running through today's stories is the erosion of online privacy and the government's growing efforts to capture and control online data. This article connects with other recent reporting that has highlighted similar attempts by the government to unmask social media users who criticize ICE or express dissenting views.

### [India has 100M weekly active ChatGPT users, Sam Altman says](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/15/india-has-100m-weekly-active-chatgpt-users-sam-altman-says/)
*techcrunch*

Today's data shows that India now boasts an astonishing 100 million weekly active ChatGPT users, making it one of OpenAI's largest markets globally.

This massive user base is driven by India's young population and over a billion internet users, which has made the country an attractive market for AI companies to fuel global expansion. TechCrunch reports that OpenAI CEO Sam Altman highlighted India as ChatGPT's second-largest user base after the United States, with students being a key growth segment.

OpenAI has adjusted its approach for India's price-sensitive market by offering a sub-$5 ChatGPT Go tier and making it free for Indian users. However, there is a broader challenge in translating widespread adoption into sustained economic impact, which Indian government initiatives aim to address.

### [The great computer science exodus (and where students are going instead)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/15/the-great-computer-science-exodus-and-where-students-are-going-instead/)
*TechCrunch*

**Key fact:** Computer science enrollment at University of California campuses has dropped 6% this year, following a decline of 3% in 2024.

This trend is significant because it signals a shift in student interest from traditional CS programs to AI-focused ones. TechCrunch reports that many universities are launching new AI-specific majors and colleges, with Chinese universities leading the way by prioritizing AI literacy as essential infrastructure. This development matters because it highlights the growing demand for skills seen as crucial in an increasingly AI-driven world.

The article's significance is further underscored by the contrast between US and Chinese university approaches to AI education. While US universities are scrambling to catch up, Chinese schools have made significant strides in integrating AI into their curricula. This discrepancy suggests that the US education system may be lagging behind in preparing students for the future of work.

The article excerpt highlights the trend, stating: "Something strange happened at University of California campuses this fall. For the first time since the dot-com crash, computer science enrollment dropped." This quote from TechCrunch underscores the unexpected nature of the decline and sets the stage for a discussion about its implications.

### [Hollywood isn’t happy about the new Seedance 2.0 video generator](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/14/hollywood-isnt-happy-about-the-new-seedance-2-0-video-generator/)
*TechCrunch*

Today's data shows that Hollywood organizations are pushing back against ByteDance's AI video model, Seedance 2.0, which they claim is being used for copyright infringement.

The article highlights a specific incident where users created unauthorized videos featuring Disney-owned characters such as Spider-Man and Baby Yoda using just a text prompt in the tool. This has led to condemnation from the Motion Picture Association, SAG-AFTRA, and other Hollywood unions, with some calling for ByteDance to cease its infringing activity.

This development is part of a broader trend where AI video generation tools are becoming increasingly popular, but also raise concerns about copyright infringement and the need for guardrails. The article notes that similar tools like OpenAI's Sora have also faced criticism for their lack of safeguards.

The thread connecting this story to other news today is the ongoing debate around the attention economy and its impact on creators' rights. As AI-generated content becomes more prevalent, it's clear that there will be a need for clearer guidelines and regulations to protect intellectual property and ensure fair compensation for creators.
