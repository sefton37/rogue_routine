These changes highlight the prioritization of national interests over international collaboration, emphasizing indigenous technological sovereignty as a key concern.

## Patterns & Signals

- **National Sovereignty Over AI Development:** Rest of World highlights how Chinese cities are incentivizing solo entrepreneurs to drive AI innovation through free apartments and data center conversions. This initiative not only democratizes access to advanced technologies but also aligns with broader national strategies aimed at fostering indigenous technological sovereignty.
  
- **Pentagon's Self-Sufficiency in AI:** TechCrunch reports that the Pentagon is developing its own AI language models instead of relying on established providers like Anthropic or OpenAI. This strategic move reflects a growing trend towards self-reliance in critical technology sectors, driven by concerns over national security and control.
  
- **Data Sharing Between Law Enforcement and Intelligence Agencies:** TechCrunch details how the Trump administration is loosening restrictions on sharing law enforcement information with intelligence agencies. While this initiative aims to enhance operational efficiency, it also raises significant privacy concerns and questions about oversight in a fragmented technological landscape.
