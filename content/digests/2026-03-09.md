### [Anthropic sues Defense Department over supply chain risk designation](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/anthropic-sues-defense-department-over-supply-chain-risk-designation/)
*TechCrunch*

According to TechCrunch, a high-stakes conflict is unfolding between Anthropic and the US Department of Defense (DOD) over access to AI systems. The DOD has labeled Anthropic as a "supply-chain risk," a designation typically reserved for foreign adversaries, due to its refusal to grant unrestricted access to its AI models for mass surveillance and autonomous weapons development.

This story is noteworthy because it reveals a power struggle between a cutting-edge tech firm and the US military over data sovereignty and control. The DOD's actions threaten to disrupt Anthropic's operations and compromise its relationships with customers, including the US military itself. As TechCrunch reports, "Anthropic called the DOD’s actions ‘unprecedented and unlawful’ in a complaint filed in San Francisco federal court." This confrontation highlights the tension between government overreach and corporate autonomy in the rapidly evolving AI landscape.

The stakes are high, as Anthropic's AI model Claude is used by various customers, including the US military. The company's CEO Dario Amodei argues that the designation is "legally unsound" and only applies to contracts with the Department of War, not all use of their AI model. This dispute has sparked an open letter signed by hundreds of tech workers urging the DOD to withdraw the designation, citing concerns over government overreach and inappropriate treatment of an American technology company.

The conflict between Anthropic and the DOD raises important questions about the limits of government power in the digital age and the rights of corporations to protect their intellectual property. As this story continues to develop, it will be essential to monitor how these two powerful entities navigate this complex and contentious issue.
