## Deep Dives

### [Delve accused of misleading customers with ‘fake compliance’](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/22/delve-accused-of-misleading-customers-with-fake-compliance/)
*TechCrunch*

TechCrunch reports that Delve, a compliance startup, is facing serious allegations of misleading hundreds of clients into believing they were compliant with privacy and security regulations like HIPAA and GDPR. An anonymous Substack author known as DeepDelver claims Delve generates fake evidence and auditor conclusions on behalf of certification mills in India, which rubber-stamp reports without independent review. This exposes customers to potential legal and financial risks if their compliance is found to be fraudulent. In response, Delve denies issuing compliance reports directly, stating it only provides an automation platform for auditors. The incident highlights growing concerns about transparency and integrity within the tech startup ecosystem, where rapid growth often prioritizes quick solutions over thorough validation.
