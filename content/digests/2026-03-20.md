### [Prediction Markets Promised Better Information. Instead They’re Creating Powerful Incentives to Corrupt Information.](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/03/19/prediction-markets-promised-better-information-instead-theyre-creating-powerful-incentives-to-corrupt-information/)
*Tech Dirt*

Tech Dirt reports that prediction markets, designed to leverage financial incentives for accurate information, have instead led to threats against journalists like Emanuel Fabian when their reporting contradicts market bets. Gamblers threatened Fabian after he reported on an Iranian missile strike in Israel, aiming to pressure him into retracting or altering his story to avoid significant losses. This incident highlights how attaching high stakes to single metrics can distort truth-seeking behavior, aligning with Goodhart's Law.

> "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure."
— [Tech Dirt](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/03/19/prediction-markets-promised-better-information-instead-theyre-creating-powerful-incentives-to-corrupt-information/)
