## The Big Picture

What happens when users mock an AI product like Microsoft’s Copilot with a term like “Microslop”? [Tech Dirt](https://techdirt.com/2026/03/15/microsoft-locks-down-discord-server-after-people-wont-stop-making-fun-of-ai-microslop/) reports that after users began mocking Microsoft’s AI products, the company locked down its Discord server to control the narrative. Similarly, [Tech Dirt](https://techdirt.com/2026/03/15/turn-out-doge-bros-who-killed-humanities-grants-are-kinda-sensitive-about-it/) details how former NEH officials used ChatGPT in a way that sparked public backlash and led to the removal of deposition videos. Both incidents highlight companies' defensive responses to user criticism, raising questions about whether such reactions hinder or facilitate product improvement.

- **Public mockery impacts corporate strategy:** Microsoft’s decision to lock down its Discord server after users mocked Copilot as “Microslop” demonstrates a company's struggle to balance public engagement with maintaining control over brand image. This defensive move contrasts with more proactive approaches that could foster better user feedback and product iteration ([Tech Dirt](https://techdirt.com/2026/03/15/microsoft-locks-down-discord-server-after-people-wont-stop-making-fun-of-ai-microslop/)).
- **Government misuse of AI sparks controversy:** The use of ChatGPT by former NEH officials to cancel humanities grants based on simplistic criteria has led to public backlash and the removal of deposition videos. This incident underscores the risks associated with deploying AI in government decision-making processes without thorough oversight, potentially undermining trust in both technology and governance ([Tech Dirt](https://techdirt.com/2026/03/15/turn-out-doge-bros-who-killed-humanities-grants-are-kinda-sensitive-about-it/)).

- **Philanthropy trends among tech leaders:** The decline in participation of the Giving Pledge, initiated by Warren Buffett and Bill Gates to encourage billionaires to donate at least half their wealth, reflects a shift towards more libertarian values focused on business success rather than societal impact. This trend challenges the notion that philanthropic efforts can effectively address wealth inequality ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/15/the-billionaires-made-a-promise-now-some-want-out/)).
- **AI investment in open-source models:** Despite limited financial incentives, businesses continue to invest heavily in open-source AI models due to the potential for early influence and mindshare. However, this strategy may face challenges as performance limitations in closed labs widen the gap between open and proprietary models ([Interconnects](https://pluralistic.net/2026/03/16/whittle-a-webserver/)).
