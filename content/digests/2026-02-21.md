### [Court Orders Slavery Exhibit At George Washington’s House Restored After Trump Admin Pulled It Down](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/02/20/court-orders-slavery-exhibit-at-george-washingtons-house-restored-after-trump-admin-pulled-it-down/)
*Tech Dirt*

The most important fact or development is that a US court has ordered the restoration of a slavery exhibit at George Washington's former Philadelphia home after the Trump administration removed it.

This ruling marks a significant setback for the Trump administration's efforts to erase inconvenient historical facts, as Tech Dirt reports. The exhibit in question referenced 9 slaves owned by Washington who spent time at the house, highlighting the complex and often uncomfortable history of American slavery. By removing this exhibit, the Trump administration was attempting to downplay or even erase this aspect of American history.

However, the court has now ruled that the federal government acted outside its authority in removing the exhibit without the city's agreement. This decision is based on agreements between the city and federal governments regarding the curation of historical sites, which Tech Dirt found were established decades ago.

The significance of this ruling lies in its implications for the power dynamics at play in American history and culture. By acknowledging the existence and importance of slavery in America's past, the court has effectively pushed back against the Trump administration's attempts to rewrite or erase that history. This decision also highlights the ongoing struggle between historical preservation and efforts to manipulate or distort the truth.

It's worth noting what this article does not reveal: it does not delve into the motivations behind the Trump administration's actions or explore the broader implications of their efforts to control the narrative around American history. Additionally, while Tech Dirt provides a critical perspective on these events, its framing focuses primarily on the Trump administration's actions and the court's decision, rather than exploring the systemic issues at play.

### [Open Letter To Tech Companies: Protect Your Users From Lawless DHS Subpoenas](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/02/20/open-letter-to-tech-companies-protect-your-users-from-lawless-dhs-subpoenas/)
*Tech Dirt*

The Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) has sent an open letter to major tech companies, including Meta and Google, urging them to protect their users from lawless administrative subpoenas issued by the Department of Homeland Security (DHS). The EFF argues that these subpoenas are often used to target individuals engaged in First Amendment activity, such as documenting ICE activities or attending protests. According to Tech Dirt, the EFF notes that DHS has issued thousands of administrative subpoenas in the past year, many of which have been used to target protected speech.

The EFF is asking tech companies to insist on court intervention before complying with subpoenas, give users notice when they are targeted, and resist gag orders that prevent them from notifying their users. This is significant because it highlights the potential for government overreach and the need for tech companies to prioritize user protection. The EFF's efforts aim to ensure that users' rights are respected and that they are not unfairly targeted by the government.

The article also reveals that DHS has withdrawn subpoenas in the past when challenged in court, indicating that these subpoenas may be unlawful. This suggests that the government is aware of the potential issues with its subpoena process but is still using it to target individuals. The EFF's efforts to push back against this practice are crucial in protecting users' rights and holding the government accountable.

### [Bondi Bragged About Forcing Facebook To Censor Speech. Now FIRE Is Suing](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/02/20/bondi-bragged-about-forcing-facebook-to-censor-speech-now-fire-is-suing/)
*Tech Dirt*

Key fact: Attorney General Pam Bondi and DHS Secretary Kristi Noem allegedly coerced social media companies to disable a 100,000-member Facebook group and an app documenting ICE enforcement activity.

This matters because government officials publicly took credit for the takedown, with Bondi admitting to a clear First Amendment violation on social media. The lawsuit filed by FIRE alleges that Bondi and Noem's actions were a direct infringement of the plaintiffs' freedom of speech.

The article highlights how government officials can manipulate social media platforms to suppress speech, which is a significant concern in the attention extraction economy. This situation shows how systemic design and incentive architecture can be exploited for political gain.

This move is seen as an attempt by Trump to seize election power from the states and ensure a Republican majority in Congress. The article highlights the erosion of democratic principles in the US, as Trump seeks to undermine the electoral process and consolidate power in the federal government. ### [At the World’s Largest General Science Meeting, Surviving Trump Is the Topic](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/02/20/at-the-worlds-largest-general-science-meeting-surviving-trump-is-the-topic/)
*Tech Dirt*

The American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS) meeting highlighted the devastating impact of Trump's policies on the US science sector. 7,800 research grants have been terminated or frozen, and 25,000 scientists and personnel have been laid off from research agencies. The proposed budget cuts of 35% to federal science funding amount to $32 billion.

These cuts have upended America's leadership role in global science, concentrating power within the federal government. Experts discussed ways for state-level scientists to fill in the gaps left by federal cuts and build public trust in science at the local level.

The article doesn't reveal what specific actions individual states are taking to address these budget cuts or how they plan to maintain scientific research and development.

The US government is planning to launch a program called Tech Corps, which will send volunteers abroad to promote American artificial intelligence (AI) and counter China's growing influence in the field. This initiative aims to provide technical assistance and training on US-developed AI technologies to countries where AI is being used for export, such as India and Southeast Asia.

The significance of this move lies in its attempt to address the global AI market dominance by China. Rest of World reports that the US government sees Tech Corps as a key component of its strategy to promote American innovation and competitiveness in the face of growing competition from China.
