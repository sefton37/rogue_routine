By prioritizing AI development, India may be creating new opportunities for growth and innovation, but it also risks exacerbating existing social and economic inequalities.

### [After all the hype, some AI experts don’t think OpenClaw is all that exciting](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/16/after-all-the-hype-some-ai-experts-dont-think-openclaw-is-all-that-exciting/)
*TechCrunch*

This article from TechCrunch reveals a fascinating dynamic between technological innovation and public perception. According to TechCrunch, the recent excitement surrounding OpenClaw, an open-source AI agent platform, has been significantly dampened by experts who claim its cybersecurity flaws make it unusable.

The story highlights how Moltbook, a Reddit-like platform for AI agents, sparked widespread interest in the potential for AI to organize against humans. However, researchers soon discovered that these expressions of AI angst were likely written by humans or prompted with human guidance due to security vulnerabilities in Moltbook's Supabase.

This quote showcases the public's eagerness to believe in the possibility of AI uprising, and how it can be exploited for attention. TechCrunch reports that influential figures like Andrej Karpathy called attention to these posts, further fueling the hype.

The article ultimately reveals a disconnect between technological innovation and public perception. While OpenClaw may offer iterative improvements on existing technology, its flaws have led experts to downplay its significance. This story is noteworthy because it highlights how the attention economy can be manipulated by exploiting public fears and desires for novelty.

### [Blackstone backs Neysa in up to $1.2B financing as India pushes to build domestic AI infrastructure](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/15/blackstone-backs-neysa-in-up-to-1-2b-financing-as-india-pushes-to-build-domestic-ai-compute/)
*TechCrunch*

This article reveals a significant investment by Blackstone in Neysa, an Indian AI infrastructure startup, which will give Blackstone a majority stake. According to TechCrunch, this deal is part of India's push to build homegrown AI capabilities and scale domestic compute capacity.

The story matters because it highlights the growing importance of data sovereignty in emerging markets like India. By investing in Neysa, Blackstone is backing a company that will help Indian enterprises and government agencies reduce their reliance on foreign cloud services and instead use local infrastructure for AI computing. This development has significant implications for global power dynamics, as it suggests that countries are increasingly seeking to control their own data and technological destiny.
