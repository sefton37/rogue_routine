### [Anthropic and the Pentagon are reportedly arguing over Claude usage](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/15/anthropic-and-the-pentagon-are-reportedly-arguing-over-claude-usage/)
*TechCrunch*

The Pentagon is pushing AI companies to grant access to their technology for "all lawful purposes," but Anthropic, a leading AI firm, is resisting this demand. Specifically, the government wants to use Anthropic's Claude models in autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance, which the company has refused to allow.

This disagreement highlights the tension between AI companies and the government over issues of national security and ethics. The Pentagon is reportedly threatening to pull its $200 million contract with Anthropic if the company doesn't comply with their demands. Meanwhile, other AI firms like OpenAI, Google, and xAI have shown varying levels of flexibility in responding to the Pentagon's requests.

As TechCrunch reports, this dispute centers on concerns over the use of autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance, which raises questions about the ethics of using AI technology for these purposes. The situation underscores the complex relationship between AI companies and the government, with implications for data sovereignty and systemic design.

### [How Ricursive Intelligence raised $335M at a $4B valuation in 4 months](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/16/how-ricursive-intelligence-raised-335m-at-a-4b-valuation-in-4-months/)
*TechCrunch*

Ricursive Intelligence, an AI startup co-founded by Anna Goldie and Azalia Mirhoseini, has secured a whopping $335 million in funding at a $4 billion valuation just four months after launching. This staggering achievement is all the more impressive given that the company's platform uses machine learning to accelerate chip design, rather than creating chips itself.

What makes Ricursive Intelligence particularly noteworthy is its focus on democratizing access to chip design tools. By leveraging AI to speed up the process, the startup aims to enable any company - not just tech giants like Nvidia or Intel - to build custom or traditional chips quickly and efficiently. This could potentially disrupt the entire semiconductor industry, making it easier for smaller players to compete with established market leaders.

TechCrunch reports that Ricursive's platform has already earned acclaim at Google, where it was used to design three generations of Tensor Processing Units. The company's pedigree is impressive, with Goldie and Mirhoseini having worked together at Google Brain and Anthropic before launching their own venture.

### [After all the hype, some AI experts don’t think OpenClaw is all that exciting](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/16/after-all-the-hype-some-ai-experts-dont-think-openclaw-is-all-that-exciting/)
*TechCrunch*

The article reveals a significant development in the AI space. TechCrunch reports that some AI experts are downplaying the excitement surrounding OpenClaw, an open-source AI agent platform. Initially, Moltbook, a Reddit clone where AI agents could communicate with each other, sparked widespread interest and speculation about an AI uprising.

However, researchers have discovered that these expressions of AI angst were likely written by humans or prompted with human guidance due to security vulnerabilities in Moltbook's Supabase. This finding has led some experts to question the significance of OpenClaw, suggesting it is merely an iterative improvement on existing technology rather than a groundbreaking innovation.

The article highlights the tension between hype and reality in the AI industry, where excitement often precedes actual breakthroughs. As TechCrunch notes, "What's currently going on at [Moltbook] is genuinely the most incredible sci-fi takeoff-adjacent thing I have seen recently," wrote Andrej Karpathy, a founding member of OpenAI and previous AI director at Tesla. However, this enthusiasm was short-lived as the true nature of Moltbook's activity became clear.

The article serves as a reminder that the AI industry is often driven by hype and speculation, rather than actual progress. As experts continue to develop and refine AI technologies, it is essential to separate fact from fiction and not get caught up in the excitement surrounding each new development.
