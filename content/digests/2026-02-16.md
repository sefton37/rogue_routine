### [Anthropic and the Pentagon are reportedly arguing over Claude usage](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/15/anthropic-and-the-pentagon-are-reportedly-arguing-over-claude-usage/)
*TechCrunch*

According to TechCrunch, a high-stakes dispute is unfolding between AI company Anthropic and the US military's Defense Department. The Pentagon wants access to Anthropic's powerful language model, Claude, for use in various operations, but Anthropic is pushing back against these demands.

This story matters because it reveals the complex power dynamics at play between tech companies and government agencies. On one hand, the Pentagon is using its significant financial leverage – a $200 million contract with Anthropic – to try and secure access to cutting-edge AI technology. On the other hand, Anthropic is resisting these demands due to concerns over the potential misuse of Claude for autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance.

As TechCrunch reports, this disagreement highlights the tension between AI companies and the government over issues of national security and ethics. The fact that Anthropic has refused to grant permission for its technology to be used in certain ways suggests a willingness to challenge the Pentagon's authority and protect its intellectual property. This development is noteworthy because it underscores the growing importance of data sovereignty and the need for tech companies to prioritize transparency and accountability in their dealings with government agencies.

This story is noteworthy because it highlights the tension between technological advancements and the public's perception of them. The article reveals how easily hype can be generated around new technologies, only to be later revealed as overblown or even fabricated. This phenomenon speaks to the power dynamics at play in the tech industry, where companies and researchers often create a sense of urgency and importance around their work.

This quote from Andrej Karpathy captures the essence of the hype surrounding Moltbook and OpenClaw. However, as the article reveals, this excitement was short-lived and ultimately misplaced. The story serves as a reminder that we must be cautious when evaluating new technologies and their potential impact on society.
