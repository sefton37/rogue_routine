### [Speaking Freely: Shin Yang](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/03/speaking-freely-shin-yang)
*EFF*

Shin Yang, a queer writer and community architect, discusses platform governance and its intersection with freedom of expression in an interview with EFF's Speaking Freely series. Yang emphasizes the importance of creating spaces where people can speak freely without fear of extraction or exposure, citing personal experiences and stories from Taiwan's sexual minority community.

Yang highlights the limitations of mainstream social media platforms in providing safe spaces for marginalized groups and advocates for well-governed online communities that prioritize mutual support and infrastructure. She focuses on building "ecospace" designed to make survival and growth possible for sexual minorities.

As Yang explains, freedom of expression is about possibility, containing both beautiful and brutal aspects. She emphasizes the need for equipment and principles that allow people to grow over time, including self-discipline, mutual support, and governance that keeps a workable gray space open for mistakes, learning, and repair.

Yang's personal experiences in Taiwan's digital ecosystem, where local independent bulletin boards were shut down due to lack of income from advertisements, have shaped her views on platform governance. She advocates for well-governed online communities that prioritize the needs of marginalized groups over profit.

EFF reports that Yang has spent a decade working anonymously building systems and designing governance protocols to create safe spaces for sexual minorities in Taiwan. Her work highlights the importance of community architecture and infrastructure in creating environments where people can speak freely without fear of extraction or exposure.

Yang's emphasis on ecospace, anonymity, minimal data collection, and behavior-based accountability resonates with themes of democratization, systemic design, and power consolidation. Her focus on building sustainable online communities that prioritize mutual support and infrastructure underscores the need for alternative models to mainstream social media platforms.

As Yang notes, freedom of expression is not about pushing discomfort out of the room but rather about creating spaces where people can grow over time. Her work serves as a model for community-driven platform governance and highlights the importance of prioritizing marginalized voices in online communities.
