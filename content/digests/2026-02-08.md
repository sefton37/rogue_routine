## The Big Picture
While New York's proposed moratorium on new data centers aims to mitigate environmental concerns by slowing down unbridled expansion, India is taking a contrasting approach with its extension of startup benefits for deep tech companies, signaling a fundamental shift in how regulatory bodies prioritize digital growth.

## Deep Dives

### [New York lawmakers propose a three-year pause on new data centers](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/07/new-york-lawmakers-propose-a-three-year-pause-on-new-data-centers/)
*TechCrunch*

Key fact: New York state lawmakers have proposed a three-year moratorium on new data center construction and operation.

This move reflects growing concerns about the rapid expansion of data centers driven by AI infrastructure investments, which are expected to increase energy bills and potentially harm the environment. TechCrunch reports that this is not an isolated effort, with at least five other states considering similar pauses.

### [India has changed its startup rules for deep tech](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/07/india-has-changed-its-startup-rules-for-deep-tech/)
*TechCrunch*

The Indian government has doubled the period for which deep tech companies are treated as startups to 20 years, allowing them more time to mature and develop commercial products.

This change aims to align policy timelines with the long development cycles of science- and engineering-led businesses, such as those in space, semiconductors, and biotech. As TechCrunch reports, the government is also mobilizing public capital through a ₹1 trillion Research, Development and Innovation Fund (RDI) to address funding gaps in early-stage deep tech companies.

By supporting deep tech startups with longer-term policy benefits and increased funding, India's government is creating a more favorable ecosystem for these high-risk, high-reward ventures.
