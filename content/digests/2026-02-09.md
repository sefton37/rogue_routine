This story is noteworthy because it highlights the vulnerability of surveillance vendors' cybersecurity and the consequences of using stalkerware apps. The article also sheds light on the power dynamics at play, where individuals are paying for tools that enable them to secretly monitor others. As TechCrunch reports, this is not an isolated incident - dozens of stalkerware apps have been hacked or exposed data in the past few years.

The hacktivist's claim that they found a "trivial" bug in Struktura's website raises concerns about the lax security measures in place to protect sensitive customer information. This incident serves as a reminder that the use of surveillance apps can have far-reaching consequences, including the potential for data breaches and exploitation of personal information.

### [Op-ed: Weakening Section 230 Would Chill Online Speech](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/02/op-ed-weakening-section-230-would-chill-online-speech)
*EFF*

This article highlights the significance of Section 230, a law that has been instrumental in preserving free expression online. According to EFF, weakening this provision would have a chilling effect on online speech, leading to massive depublication of content and stifling real-time user-generated speech.

The author argues that Congress' deliberate choice of immunity in Section 230 has enabled millions of people to publish their thoughts, photos, and jokes online without fear of liability. This suggests that the law is not just a shield for "big tech" companies, but also a crucial safeguard for individual freedom of expression on the internet.

The article reveals how the power dynamics between governments, corporations, and individuals are playing out in the digital realm. It highlights the tension between those who want to hold online services accountable for user-generated content and those who see Section 230 as essential for preserving free speech online. The EFF's stance underscores the importance of protecting this provision, which has been a cornerstone of internet freedom for three decades.

### [Discord to roll out age verification next month for full access to its platform](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/09/discord-to-roll-out-age-verification-next-month-for-full-access-to-its-platform/)
*TechCrunch*

According to TechCrunch, Discord is introducing global age verification starting next month, requiring users to prove they're adults to access certain settings and age-restricted content. This move follows similar efforts by other online platforms like Roblox and YouTube to strengthen child safety.

What's noteworthy about this story is that it highlights the trade-offs between safety measures and individual freedoms. Discord claims that user data will be deleted quickly after age confirmation, but a previous security incident highlighted vulnerabilities in its third-party vendor relationships. This raises concerns about data protection and potential biases in facial age estimation solutions used for verification.

The article reveals that Discord's age verification process involves either completing a facial age estimation or submitting an ID to the platform's vendor partners. The company plans to add more options in the future, but this current setup has sparked debate about the balance between safety and individual rights. As TechCrunch notes, users will be defaulted into a "teen-appropriate experience" unless they complete age verification, which may limit their online interactions.

The story is also significant because it shows how tech companies are responding to growing international efforts to strengthen child safety online. By requiring age verification, Discord aims to reduce the risk of minors accessing mature content or interacting with strangers online. However, this move has sparked concerns about data protection and potential biases in facial age estimation solutions used for verification.

By exporting jobs to countries with more lenient regulations, these companies are effectively circumventing immigration policies that limit their access to foreign workers.

The article suggests that this trend may be a long-term consequence of the Trump administration's policies on immigration and visa restrictions. This shift has significant implications for the global tech industry, as it highlights the tension between national borders and the free flow of talent in the digital economy.
