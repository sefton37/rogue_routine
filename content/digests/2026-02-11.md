### [Can India be a “third way” AI alternative to the U.S. and China?](https://restofworld.org/2026/india-ai-summit-third-way-global-south-big-tech/?utm_source=rss&utm_medium=rss&utm_campaign=feeds)
*Rest of World*

India is hosting an AI Impact Summit, aiming to give developing countries a platform to shape the future of artificial intelligence. This event marks a significant development in the global AI landscape, as India seeks to establish itself as a major player alongside the US and China.

The summit's focus on democratizing AI and giving voice to the Global South suggests that India is trying to create an alternative ecosystem that balances power and data concentration. By promoting its own AI ecosystem and fostering innovation with other developing countries, India hopes to reduce dependence on dominant tech companies from the US and China.

Rest of World reports that this move comes amidst growing concerns about the concentration of power and data in the hands of a few large tech companies. The emphasis on collaboration and innovation with Global South countries implies that India is trying to consolidate its own influence and create opportunities for emerging economies.

Per Rest of World, the AI Impact Summit can be seen as an attempt by India to carve out a "third way" in the global AI landscape, distinct from the US-China duopoly. This development has implications for data sovereignty, as it may lead to more diverse and decentralized AI systems that better serve the needs of emerging economies.

As Rest of World details, this move also reflects growing tensions between the Global North and South in the digital economy. The summit's focus on democratizing AI and promoting innovation with developing countries highlights the need for a more inclusive and equitable approach to technological development.

### [No One, Including Our Furry Friends, Will Be Safer in Ring’s Surveillance Nightmare](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/02/no-one-including-our-furry-friends-will-be-safer-rings-surveillance-nightmare-0)
*EFF*

Amazon Ring's Super Bowl ad showcased a vision of widespread surveillance, where its doorbell cameras could identify and track individuals, pets, and objects using AI analysis. This is not the first time Ring has faced criticism for its handling of user data and collaboration with law enforcement. The company has a history of privacy violations, enabling surveillance of innocents and protesters, and has made law enforcement access a regular part of its business.

EFF reports that Amazon Ring's "Search Party" feature allows users to scan footage across multiple devices in a neighborhood, raising concerns about biometric identification and data collection. This feature can lead to unintended consequences, such as the erosion of privacy and increased surveillance of communities. As EFF details, Ring has already integrated biometric identification into its products via features like face recognition, which could run afoul of biometric privacy laws in some states.

The article highlights how Ring's features can be used for mass surveillance, enabling law enforcement to track individuals without their consent. This is a significant concern, as it raises questions about the company's commitment to user privacy and its willingness to collaborate with authorities. The EFF notes that users can disable the "Search Party" feature by following specific steps in the app, but this does not address the broader issue of Ring's surveillance practices.
