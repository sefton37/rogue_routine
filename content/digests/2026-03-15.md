## The Big Picture

Over 75% of Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests are either denied or significantly delayed by federal agencies, according to the "Foilies 2026" awards from EFF and MuckRock. This statistic highlights a systemic failure in government transparency that erodes public trust and accountability, with far-reaching implications for democratic processes and civic engagement. However, today's coverage largely avoids delving into how these delays affect democratic processes and civic engagement beyond highlighting specific instances of obstruction. The articles focus on individual cases rather than broader implications, such as the cumulative impact on citizens' ability to hold their elected officials accountable or the systemic reasons behind widespread transparency failures.

## Deep Dives

### [The Foilies 2026](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/03/foilies-2026)
*EFF*

"The Foilies 2026," an annual project by EFF and MuckRock, recognizes government entities that obstruct public access to information through Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests. This year's awards highlight officials like Texas Governor Greg Abbott for withholding emails with Elon Musk and Vancouver City Council for charging fees for FOIA requests, showcasing systemic issues in transparency.

The article does not delve into the broader implications of these delays and obstructions on democratic processes or public trust. It also avoids questioning why certain entities are targeted while others receive praise, potentially reflecting EFF's advocacy focus rather than a comprehensive analysis of government transparency issues.
